Terms and Conditions

By participating in Hack Celestial 2024, all participants agree to abide by the following terms and conditions. These rules ensure a fair, organized, and positive environment for all participants throughout the event.

Eligibility

  • The hackathon is open to all full-time students, regardless of their specialization, from recognized educational institutions in India.
  • Teams must consist of 4-5 members, with a mandatory requirement of at least 1 female participant per team.
  • Participants from various academic disciplines and years of study are encouraged to join, promoting interdisciplinary collaboration.

Team Composition

  • Each participant can be a part of only one team.
  • Intercollege teams are not permitted; all team members must be from the same institution.
  • There is no limit on the number of teams that can register from the same institute, ensuring wide participation.
  • Teams with a mix of members from different academic backgrounds are encouraged to bring diverse skill sets to the competition.

Project Development

  • Teams are expected to work on problem statements provided at the start of the hackathon. Projects must be original and developed during the event.
  • All submissions must include original code and designs, and any use of third-party resources (open-source tools or libraries) should be disclosed and appropriately credited.
  • Pre-existing codebases, templates, or projects are strictly prohibited unless specified by the organizers.

Participation Guidelines

  • Teams are required to work collaboratively throughout the 48-hour event and must adhere to the timeline and submission deadlines.
  • Teams may not add or remove members once registration is completed and approved.
  • Each team is responsible for the confidentiality of their project. The organizing committee will not be liable for any intellectual property disputes arising from team collaboration or external sharing.

Judging and Evaluation

  • Teams will be judged based on innovation, technical execution, feasibility, user experience, and presentation quality..
  • All decisions made by the panel of judges are final and binding.
  • Teams must submit their final projects by the designated deadline; late submissions will not be considered unless granted special permission by the organizing committee.

Code of Conduct

  • All participants must maintain professionalism, courtesy, and integrity throughout the hackathon.
  • Discriminatory, offensive, or disruptive behavior, including any form of harassment, will result in immediate disqualification.
  • Participants must respect the event's rules, as well as the privacy and intellectual property of other teams.

Disqualification

  • Violation of any rules outlined in these terms and conditions may lead to disqualification at the discretion of the organizers.
  • Any team found to be using unfair means or attempting to plagiarize or copy the work of others will be immediately disqualified.

Prizes and Recognition

  • Prizes will be awarded to top-performing teams based on the evaluation criteria set by the organizers and judges.
  • Prize distribution and decisions regarding winners will be final, with no provision for dispute.

Intellectual Property

  • Teams retain full ownership of their projects, including code and designs, developed during the hackathon.
  • By participating, teams grant the organizers the right to showcase their projects for promotional purposes, while proper credit is always given to the team members involved.

Liability

  • The organizers are not responsible for any technical issues, hardware failures, or loss of data during the hackathon.
  • Participants agree to use their own resources and tools while adhering to the rules of the event.

By registering and participating in Hack Celestial 2024, all participants confirm that they have read, understood, and accepted these terms and conditions.
